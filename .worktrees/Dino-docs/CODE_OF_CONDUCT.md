# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, the DINOForge community, are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political beliefs, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We expect all members of the community to behave professionally and treat one another with dignity and respect.

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

- **Be respectful**: Treat others with kindness and empathy
- **Be collaborative**: Work together constructively toward common goals
- **Be honest**: Provide accurate information and acknowledge mistakes
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Be constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and criticism
- **Respect boundaries**: Honor others' time, privacy, and personal limits
- **Follow project guidelines**: Adhere to CONTRIBUTING.md and technical standards

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any context:

- **Harassment or discrimination** based on any identity characteristic
- **Hate speech, slurs, or offensive language** targeting individuals or groups
- **Intentional disruption** of discussions, code review, or project workflows
- **Trolling, baiting, or bad-faith engagement** designed to provoke or offend
- **Doxxing** or publishing others' private information without consent
- **Threats or violence** of any kind, implied or direct
- **Sexual harassment** or unwanted sexual attention
- **Exclusionary behavior** designed to push people out of the community
- **Unwanted advertising** or spam

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all DINOForge community spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Our official communication channels
- In-person events and conferences
- Unofficial community spaces (forums, Discord, etc.) where DINOForge is discussed

Maintainers may extend or modify this scope as needed.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it to the maintainers:

- **Email**: Contact the project owner via GitHub
- **GitHub**: Report via private message to a maintainer
- **Issue**: For serious violations, create a private security advisory

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- Who was involved (if safe to share)
- Any relevant links or context
- How you'd like the situation resolved

Reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly.

### Consequences

Depending on severity, consequences may include:

1. **Warning**: First-time minor violations may result in a private warning
2. **Temporary ban**: Persistent violations may result in temporary suspension
3. **Permanent ban**: Serious or repeated violations may result in removal from the community

All enforcement decisions are made in good faith with the goal of maintaining a healthy community.

## Appeals

If you believe you've been unfairly treated, you may appeal to the project maintainers. Appeals should include:

- Your name and the incident reference
- Why you believe the decision was unjust
- What you'd like to see changed

Appeals will be reviewed by a maintainer not involved in the original decision.
